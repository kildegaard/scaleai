3D Geometric Figures

Cube

A cube is a three-dimensional solid object bounded by six square faces, with three meeting at each vertex.

Volume Formula:

V = a3 (where 'a' is the length of one side)

Sphere

A sphere is a perfectly round geometrical object in three-dimensional space, that is, the surface of a completely round ball.

Volume Formula:

V = (4/3)πr3 (where 'r' is the radius)

Pyramid

A pyramid is a polyhedron formed by connecting a polygonal base and a point, called the apex. Each base edge and apex form a triangle, called a lateral face.

Volume Formula:

V = (1/3)Bh (where 'B' is the area of the base and 'h' is the height)

Cylinder

A cylinder is a three-dimensional solid that holds two parallel bases joined by a curved surface, at a fixed distance.

Volume Formula:

V = πr2h (where 'r' is the radius and 'h' is the height)

Cone

A cone is a three-dimensional geometric shape that tapers smoothly from a flat base to a point called the apex or vertex.

Volume Formula:

V = (1/3)πr2h (where 'r' is the radius and 'h' is the height)
